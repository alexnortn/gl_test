pd-dual · the hero piece

What you're looking at

pd-dual places two real neurons in the same 3D space, exactly where they sit in the retina, and lets you watch signals move through them.

Cell 10010 — ganglion cell Cell 70014 — starburst amacrine cell (SAC) 22 shared synapses

The two cells touch at 22 synaptic contacts (the gold spheres), drawn directly from the connectome's contact data. Signals propagate in the biologically correct direction: the starburst amacrine cell fires outward from its soma, an expanding wave that branches down every dendrite to the tips; the ganglion cell fires inward, a pulse travelling back along its arbor toward the cell body. When a starburst wave reaches a shared synapse, that contact flares and hands the signal across to the ganglion cell — the circuit, firing.

The neuroscience

Starburst amacrine cells (SACs) are central to how the retina detects the direction of motion. Their radial dendrites are presynaptic to direction-selective ganglion cells, and the fine structure of that wiring — which parts of a SAC dendrite connect to which ganglion cell, and with what timing — is what lets the circuit distinguish motion one way from the other. Mapping that wiring at the level of individual synapses is exactly what connectomics makes possible.

📄 The paper

This circuitry is the subject of “Space-time wiring specificity supports direction selectivity in the retina” (Kim et al., Nature, 2014) — work I contributed to during my time in Sebastian Seung's computational neuroscience lab. The neuron reconstructions shown here come from that lineage of work.

🎮 EyeWire

The meshes were reconstructed through EyeWire, the citizen-science game from the Seung lab where hundreds of thousands of players traced retinal neurons through electron-microscopy data — another project I worked on back then. Every dendrite you see here was mapped by that community.

Tip: in the full visualization, click a cell or synapse to fire a pulse, switch on Circuit or Auto mode to let it run, and use the snap-views and auto-orbit to explore.
